HITS Algorithm

The HITS Algorithm is the precursor to the famous Google PageRank. The main idea is that--for a given search query--some websites are authorities on the query, and others are hubs that link to the best authorities. (Some are both).

Given a query, first use text-based techniques, and assign each page a hub and authority value. These values start at 1, and are updated incrementally as follows:

  • hub(page) = sum of authorty values of outgoing links
  • authority(page) = sum of hub values of incoming links
  • normalize hub and authority values to sum to 1
After many such rounds, the scores will begin to converge. This is a great example of mutual recursion.
